The Website · Explained

The One Idea

Light is the agent of reunion.

A Northern-European family lost the light — and in the grey, lost each other to commutes, screens and short dark days. This home gives both back. The sun moves through the house and gathers the family, room by room, across the day.

Speak to “you” Make them feel, not admire Show the ache before the relief

The Signature Interaction

The grey→gold light arc.

One variable — --warmth — runs 0 → 1 with scroll and blends every section background from cool mist toward warm cream. Descending the page is descending from dawn into golden hour.

03 — Midday · The Salón-Comedor

Where the sun gathers everyone.

The open-plan living-dining room — ~37m², the house's emotional heart — where the family is simply in the same warm room without trying to be. The one room the whole site builds toward.

04 — Afternoon · Terrace & Garden

The light spills outdoors, and the afternoon refuses to hurry.

Private garden, room for a table where the sun lands longest. Villa 39 — the hero unit — keeps it all day.

05 — Evening · The Bedrooms

When the light goes low, the house keeps the warmth it gathered.

Three bedrooms on the first floor, plus a flexible basement room — an optional fourth bed or guest suite. Deep, quiet, made for rest.

06 — The Invitation · The Close

Come and stand in the light yourself.

A quiet lead-capture form — name, email, unit of interest, preferred date — gated on “Request the brochure.” No price appears anywhere on the site; every CTA is the brochure.
